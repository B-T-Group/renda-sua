2. Merchant obligations

You agree to: (a) provide accurate, clear product descriptions, prices, and images; (b) honour confirmed orders in a timely manner; (c) maintain adequate stock and update availability; (d) provide responsive customer service; (e) comply with consumer protection, tax, and business registration requirements in your jurisdiction.

3. Acceptable use

You must not list or sell illegal, counterfeit, stolen, dangerous, or prohibited goods; content that is fraudulent, misleading, defamatory, or hateful; or products that infringe intellectual property. 4. Product information standards

Listings must truthfully represent the item, including condition, quantity, allergens or safety warnings where relevant, and delivery or pickup terms. Photos should reflect the actual product offered.

Applicable commissions, delivery fees, and payout schedules are disclosed in the Platform or separate fee schedules. You are solely responsible for declaring and remitting taxes owed on your sales.

6. Data protection

You will handle customer data received through the Platform only for order fulfilment and as permitted by our privacy policy and applicable law. You must not misuse or resell customer personal data.

7. Suspension and termination

We may suspend or terminate your merchant access for breach of this Agreement, legal risk, or repeated customer complaints. You may stop using the Platform by closing your account subject to outstanding obligations.

8. Limitation of liability

To the fullest extent permitted by law, Rendasua is not liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages arising from your use of the Platform. Our aggregate liability is limited to fees paid by you to us in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim, except where law prohibits such limitation.

This Agreement is governed by the laws of Cameroon and applicable OHADA commercial principles, without regard to conflict-of-law rules. Disputes shall be submitted to the competent courts of Douala, Cameroon, unless mandatory consumer or local law requires otherwise.
